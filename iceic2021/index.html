Abstract

This paper establishes a mathematical model for multi-view thermal camera calibration using shared geometric constraints. We address the challenge of calibrating thermal cameras that have limited overlap between views (greater than 180-degree separation) by leveraging vanishing point geometry and homography relationships.

Key Contributions

  • Mathematical Framework: Novel formulation for multi-view thermal camera calibration with minimal correspondence points
  • Geometric Constraints: Exploitation of vanishing points and parallel line structures in thermal images
  • Wide-Angle Configuration: Support for camera setups with >180Â° total field of view separation
  • Reduced Correspondences: Calibration achievable with significantly fewer point correspondences than traditional methods

Methodology

The proposed approach establishes geometric relationships between multiple thermal camera views through:

  1. Vanishing point detection in each thermal image using edge detection and line fitting
  2. Establishment of inter-camera geometric constraints via shared scene structure
  3. Joint optimization of intrinsic and extrinsic parameters across all views
  4. Refinement using bundle adjustment with thermal-specific noise models
