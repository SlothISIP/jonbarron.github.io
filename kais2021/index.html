Abstract

This paper proposes improving thermal camera calibration accuracy by enhancing image quality through deep learning-based super-resolution and filtering techniques. Thermal images typically suffer from lower resolution and higher noise compared to visible-light cameras, which degrades calibration accuracy. Our approach addresses these limitations through preprocessing enhancement.

Key Contributions

  • Image Enhancement Pipeline: Deep learning-based super-resolution tailored for thermal imagery
  • Noise Reduction: Adaptive filtering techniques preserving edge information while reducing thermal noise
  • Improved Corner Detection: Enhanced feature extraction from processed thermal images
  • Calibration Accuracy: Demonstrated reduction in reprojection error through quality improvement

Methodology

The proposed pipeline consists of:

  1. Acquisition of thermal calibration board images
  2. Deep learning super-resolution to enhance spatial resolution
  3. Bilateral filtering for noise reduction while preserving edges
  4. Enhanced corner/checkerboard detection on processed images
  5. Standard camera calibration with improved feature points
